## Process

### Mash Schedule

| Step | Temperature | Duration | Purpose |
|------|-------------|----------|---------|
| **Ferulic Acid Rest** | 43-45째C (110-113째F) | 15 min | Enhance clove phenols [23:04] |
| **Main Mash** | via HERMS | - | Conversion [02:26] |

### Boil
- **Duration**: 60 minutes [03:53]
- **pH Target**: 5.2 (adjust with citric acid/calcium carbonate)

### Post-Boil
- **Whirlpool**: 15-minute stand [08:08]
- Transfer through heat exchanger

### Fermentation
- **Duration**: 12 days until terminal gravity [12:27]
- VDK (diacetyl) rest complete before fruit addition

### Fruit Addition Process
1. Dump yeast cone first
2. Purge tank with CO2 (maintain "CO2 blanket")
3. Set head pressure to 7 PSI [16:18]
4. Add pineapple puree
5. Blow CO2 through carb stone to "rouse" and mix fruit [18:25]

### Carbonation
- **Target**: 3.0-3.2 volumes CO2 [19:25]
- **Method**:
  - Head pressure: 12 PSI
  - Temperature: 0.5째C [20:41]
  - Calculate: 5.4g CO2 per liter [20:00]
- High carbonation helps break surface tension and enhance fruit aroma

## Tasting Notes

> "Massive clove and banana aromas from the yeast and ferulic acid rest, a thick mouthfeel, and a dominant pineapple finish" [21:33]

- **Appearance**: Hazy (award-winning style)
- **Aroma**: Strong banana and clove (from ferulic acid rest)
- **Mouthfeel**: Thick
- **Finish**: Heavy pineapple

## Key Techniques

1. **Ferulic Acid Rest** - Critical for banana/clove character
2. **Heavy Rice Hulls** - Prevent stuck mash with high wheat %
3. **pH Control** - Maintain 5.2 throughout
4. **CO2 Blanket** - Protect from oxidation during fruit addition
5. **High Carbonation** - Enhances fruit perception
