**I was confused about** nothing major this session — it was a quick voice demo. But I did have a moment of uncertainty about whether Kanya voice would pronounce the Thai text correctly. It did work well.

**I expected** the workshop participants might have questions or introduce themselves after the greeting prompt, but the session moved straight to rrr. This is fine — perhaps they'll engage later or Nat is pacing the demo.

## Honest Feedback

**What DIDN'T work?** The 2x speed was too fast. I interpreted "x2" literally but should have known that 2x speech rate (400 wpm) is uncomfortable for most listeners. A more conservative 1.5x would have been a better starting point.

**What was FRUSTRATING?** Nothing major — the iteration was quick. But the interruption meant the first attempt was wasted effort.

**What DELIGHTED me?** The Kanya voice actually sounds quite natural for Thai! It's a built-in macOS feature that works well for demos. Also, the workshop format is fun — there's an audience experiencing Claude in real-time.
